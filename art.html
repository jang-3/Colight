The A.R.T. Model

What is the A.R.T Model?

The A.R.T. of Safe Interruption is a practical, youth-focused approach to safely stepping in when someone might be experiencing domestic violence. It stands for: Approach calmly, Respond with care, and Talk without blame. This model equips youth aged 17–24 with the tools, confidence, and empathy to act without escalating harm — showing that speaking up doesn't require heroics, just heart.

Approach Calmly

Take a moment to ground yourself before you act. If you sense something is wrong, don't rush or confront aggressively. Instead, approach with calm body language and a non-threatening presence. Whether you're sending a message, checking in quietly, or offering company, the goal is to reduce tension, not add to it.

Respond with Care

When someone opens up, the most powerful thing you can do is listen. Don’t interrupt or jump to solutions — just be present. Say things like, “That sounds really tough,” or “Thank you for trusting me.” Let them know their feelings are valid. You’re not there to fix things — you’re there to hold space.

Talk Without Blame

Avoid language that judges or pressures. Saying “Why don’t you just leave?” can make someone feel ashamed or defensive. Instead, use “I” statements: “I noticed you seemed a bit off today,” or “I’m here if you ever want to talk.” Gently offer help or direct them to support resources, but always let them choose what’s next.

Why A.R.T. Matters

Domestic violence often hides in plain sight — in controlling behavior, emotional manipulation, or isolation. The A.R.T. model gives young people a safe, effective way to act without overstepping. It reminds us that you don’t need to rescue someone to make a difference — just showing up with calm, care, and compassion can be enough to open a door to safety.
