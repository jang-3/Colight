About Us

Our Team

We are CoLight, a multidisciplinary student team passionate about using immersive media to advocate for social causes. Comprising designers, developers, and storytellers from Ngee Ann Polytechnic, we believe in the power of empathy, creativity, and technology to inspire change. Our work is rooted in compassion, driven by data, and informed by lived experiences — we stand against domestic violence and strive to amplify the voices of survivors and bystanders alike.

Our Mission

Our mission is to empower youth aged 17–24 to recognize that domestic violence is not a private matter, and to equip them with the tools and confidence to intervene safely using the A.R.T. of Safe Interruption model. Through a combination of immersive experiences, storytelling, and interactive digital tools, we aim to transform passive awareness into active responsibility, shifting mindsets and fostering a culture of safe intervention and support.

Our Approach

We are executing the campaign through a multi-platform strategy grounded in interactive engagement and emotional storytelling: Personality Quiz: Helps youth identify their bystander type and learn how to intervene based on their traits. WebAR Filter & AI Image Generator: Leverages immersive media to personalize intervention messages and encourage social sharing. Interactive Projection & VR Dart Game: Educates through hands-on activities at our NP booth, reinforcing safe intervention practices. Social Experiments & Public Activations: Conducted across polytechnics and Orchard Road to observe real reactions and raise awareness. Instagram & TikTok Campaigns: Distributes relatable and sharable content tied to personas and the A.R.T. model. Bingo Card Challenge: Encourages deeper participation and rewards youth for engaging with the campaign.

Why It Matters

Why this issue is important to us and our peers Domestic violence often hides behind silence and misconceptions, especially among youth who may feel it's not their place to step in. Our peers may witness signs but hesitate due to fear, uncertainty, or apathy. As young adults ourselves, we’ve seen how a lack of awareness or confidence can prevent action. This campaign matters because it equips us — and our generation — with the language, tools, and courage to break the cycle of silence. By normalizing intervention, we are fostering a safer, more supportive community for everyone.
